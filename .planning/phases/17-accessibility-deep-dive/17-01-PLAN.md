<objective>
Close out the v1.4 UI Polish milestone with a deep-dive accessibility pass. A probe against the dark-mode axe-core sweep (run before this plan) identified a single root cause behind 5 color-contrast violations: dark-mode `--color-text-secondary` is too dim and `--color-accent` is too dim for small-text contrast against card and border surfaces.

Fixing the two tokens resolves every documented violation, lets us remove the `disableRules(['color-contrast'])` escape hatch from both a11y specs, and closes A11Y-01, A11Y-02, A11Y-03, A11Y-04:
- A11Y-01 (contrast review) — documented + fixed
- A11Y-02 (screen reader) — already in good shape (aria-labelledby, live regions, role semantics are all in place from Phases 12/13; validated by existing axe rules)
- A11Y-03 (keyboard) — already in good shape (Phase 12 added visible focus-visible outlines to SelectPicker options; Phase 15 added focus-visible rings to shell tab anchors). Axe rules under `wcag2a`/`wcag2aa` catch missing focus.
- A11Y-04 (axe e2e green in both themes) — achieved by the token bumps + spec cleanup

**Probed violations (dark mode, recorded by the probe run before this plan):**

| # | Selector | Foreground | Background | Computed ratio | Need |
|---|----------|-----------|------------|----------------|------|
| 1 | `.border-transparent > span` (desktop inactive tab label) | `#80878b` (text-secondary) | `#353b3f` (surface-card behind header) | 3.11 | 4.5 |
| 2 | `.border-[var(--color-accent)] > span` (desktop active tab label) | `#00a7d2` (accent) | `#353b3f` | 4.03 | 4.5 |
| 3 | SelectPicker label span | `#80878b` | `#444b4f` | 2.43 | 4.5 |
| 4 | NumericInput `<label>` | `#80878b` | `#444b4f` | 2.43 | 4.5 |
| 5 | NumericInput suffix span | `#80878b` | `#1c2428` | 4.32 | 4.5 |

All five resolve with two token adjustments in `src/app.css` dark block:

- `--color-text-secondary: oklch(70% 0.010 228)` → `oklch(80% 0.010 228)` (lifts from L=70 to L=80)
- `--color-accent: oklch(72% 0.14 220)` → `oklch(82% 0.12 220)` (lifts lightness, slightly reduces chroma to keep perceptual warmth without breaking brand identity)

These changes stay inside the Clinical Blue hue family (220), keep the OKLCH token discipline, and are perceptually uniform with the rest of the dark palette.
</objective>
