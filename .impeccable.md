## Design Context

### Users

NICU clinicians (dietitians, nurses, GI physicians) at point of care — the primary audience. Usage is quick, focused, and often one-handed on a mobile device in a busy clinical environment. Parents/caregivers managing medically complex infants at home are a secondary audience. Both need to get an accurate calculation result in seconds, then act on it. High-stakes, interruption-prone. A wrong value has real consequences.

### Brand Personality

**Precise. Calm. Trustworthy.**

The app should feel like a warm but precise clinical tool — not a cold instrument, not a soft consumer product. It earns trust through visual restraint, typographic precision, and zero noise. Authoritative without being cold. Professional but human. Quietly confident.

### Emotional Goals

- **Clinical confidence**: The output must feel authoritative. Users must trust the number without second-guessing.
- **Calm & focused**: No visual anxiety. A quiet UI that reduces cognitive load in high-pressure moments.
- **Certainty**: Zero doubt about what the result means or what to do next.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Visual tone**: Warm clinical — slightly warmer neutrals than a pure hospital aesthetic, softer radius, approachable without being playful. Feels like a trusted reference tool, not an enterprise dashboard.
- **Color**: OKLCH color system. Clinical Blue (hue ~220) as precision accent. BMF Amber (hue ~60) for formula fortifier mode. Slate neutrals carry structure. Red reserved strictly for errors/warnings.
- **Typography**: Plus Jakarta Sans — professional, distinctive, highly legible. Large bold numerals for result values; everything else recedes. Tabular numerics for clinical outputs.
- **Theme**: Both light and dark mode, both feel intentional. Light is primary for bright NICU environments. Dark mode is a genuine alternative, not an afterthought.
- **Anti-references**:
  - NOT consumer health (no gradients, illustrations, wellness pastels)
  - NOT legacy EHR (no dense tables, grey boxes, form overload)
  - NOT SaaS dashboards (no sidebar nav, charts, enterprise grey)
  - NOT marketing pages (no hero sections, imagery, promotional copy)
  - NOT baby/parenting apps (no pastels, cartoon type, playful energy)
  - NOT invisible minimal (affordances must be clear — clinical users act without guessing)

### Design Principles

1. **The result is the interface.** The calculation output is the only thing that matters. Every design decision should make it easier to reach and read that number.

2. **Earn trust through restraint.** Clinicians trust tools that don't try too hard. Remove decoration, reduce color, let precision speak.

3. **Mobile-first, one-hand-friendly.** 48px minimum touch targets, safe-area padding, decimal keyboard, bottom-reachable interactions. Design for a hallway, not a desk.

4. **Warm restraint.** Slightly warmer than clinical, slightly cooler than consumer. Rounded corners and breathing room signal care, not clutter. No decoration without function.

5. **Accessibility is non-negotiable.** WCAG 2.1 AA contrast minimum. Visible focus states, ARIA labels, live regions, keyboard navigation. Clinicians may be distracted — the interface must handle it.

6. **Dark and light must both feel intentional.** Not just inverted — each mode should feel designed for itself. The OKLCH color system ensures perceptual consistency across both.
